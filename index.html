Software Testing Applied to Internet of Things: A Systematic Mapping



The Internet of Things (IoT) is a paradigm based on the interaction between intelligent devices (things) and physical and/or virtual resources through the Internet. These features may cause applications in this domain to become more prone to failures. In this scenario, evaluating the quality of applications through software testing for IoT can be considered critical and challenging. The focus of this work is on the collection and analysis of the studies available in the literature with the objectives of obtaining a comprehensive understanding of the adoption of Software Testing in the context of IoT, identifying gaps in existing approaches, and providing new directions for research in the area. To achieve this goal, a systematic mapping covering works published in journals and conferences was carried out, accessing five relevant electronic databases. As a result, a total of 79 studies were selected and analyzed, as they present or perform some kind of software test on IoT devices.

Keywords: Software Engineering; Software Testing; Internet of Things; Quality assessment; Systematic Mapping.

Research Questions

The objective of this work is to present an overview of the testing area in the IoT projects. Therefore, the following Research Questions (RQ) were defined:

RESEARCH QUESTIONS 1:

Which types of software tests are the MOST commonly used for IoT projects?

Rationale: This RQ aims to identify the main types of software tests applied in research in the area of IoT.

RESEARCH QUESTIONS 2:

What is the contribution level of software engineering, specifically in the scope of Software Tests, in IoT projects?

Rationale: The purpose of this RQ is to evaluate the contribution of each type of test present in articles in the IoT area

RQ1: Types of software tests MOST commonly used in IoT projects

As shown in Figure 6, it was observed that the Performance Testing was applied in 27.37% of the total primary studies, followed by the Usability Test and the Functional Test, each with 14.74 and 12.63%, respectively. In addition, System (12.63%), Stress (8.42%) and User Tests (9.47%) were also more significant in their application. The remainder of the software tests comprised of less than 20% of all primary studies. In about 17.72% of the primary studies, more than one test is applied. Figure 7 depicts a more comprehensive understanding of the relationship between the different types of software tests and identifies which types of tests are most related in the context of IoT for each selected article. For example, a key observation illustrates that the Functional and Performance Tests are the most related, sharing 4 common articles [S11, S39, S41, S43]. Another article that stands out [S66] presents four different types of test: Functional, Security, Stress and Performance.
